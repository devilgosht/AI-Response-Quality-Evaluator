# Research

## Introduction

The AI Response Quality Evaluator is a system designed to evaluate the quality of responses generated by Large Language Models (LLMs). The system measures different quality aspects such as relevance, accuracy, completeness, and hallucination while generating an explainable evaluation report.

---

## Large Language Models (LLMs)

Large Language Models are AI models trained on massive amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like responses.

Examples:
- ChatGPT
- Google Gemini
- Claude
- Llama

---

## LLM Evaluation

LLM evaluation is the process of measuring the quality of AI-generated responses using different evaluation metrics.

Evaluation includes:
- Accuracy
- Relevance
- Completeness
- Hallucination Detection

---

## Hallucination Detection

Hallucination occurs when an AI model generates information that is incorrect or unsupported by reliable sources.

The evaluator detects such responses by comparing them with trusted reference knowledge.

---

## Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Retrieval-Augmented Generation combines information retrieval with language generation.

Instead of relying only on the model's internal knowledge, the system retrieves relevant information from a knowledge base before evaluating responses.

---

## Evaluation Frameworks

This project explores the following frameworks:

- RAGAS
- TruLens

These frameworks help measure the quality and reliability of AI-generated responses.

---

## Datasets

The project uses public datasets including:

- TruthfulQA
- SQuAD

These datasets provide reference questions and answers for evaluation.
